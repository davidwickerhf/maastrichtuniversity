## Introduction
Christmas Carol is based in London during the Christmas Eve and Christmas day of [[1843]]
Written by Charles Dickens, the story was published on the 19 December 1843
In the novel, three spirits appear to the main character, Scrooge, to make him change his attitude towards Christmas and people.
 
## Main Characters
**Ebenezer Scrooge** → The miserly owner of a London counting-house, a nineteenth century term for an accountant's office. The three spirits of Christmas visit the stodgy bean-counter in hopes of reversing Scrooge's greedy, cold-hearted approach to life.

**Bob Cratchit** → Scrooge's clerk, a kind, mild, and very poor man with a large family. Though treated harshly by his boss, Cratchit remains a humble and dedicated employee. He was a really kind and poor man who loved Christmas, he had many children, one of them Tiny Tim was really sick and weak and risked to die.

**Tiny Tim** → Bob Cratchit's young son, crippled from birth. Tiny Tim is a highly sentimentalized character who Dickens uses to highlight the tribulations of England's poor and to elicit sympathy from his middle and upper class readership.

**Jacob Marley** → In the living world, Ebenezer Scrooge's equally greedy partner. Marley died seven years before the narrative opens. He appears to Scrooge as a ghost condemned to wander the world bound in heavy chains. Marley hopes to save his old partner from suff ering a similar fate.

**Signora Dilibert** → she was Scrooge’s maid

**Fred** → Scrooge's nephew, he loved his uncle but he also wanted his capital for paying his debts

**Spirit of present Christmas** → he showed Scrooge the present Christmas, how poor some people were and how people were making fun of him (his nephew). He showed him how Bob’s family was spending the Christmas and hoe sick Tim was

**Spirit of past Christmas** → he showed Scrooge his youth in his lil town, he used to be happy, he also met a really pretty girl, but then he changed and started caring too much about earning money so she decided do release him from their promise and left him

**Spirit of future Christmas** → he showed Scrooge the thing that were about to happen during Christmas day, Scrooge was death and nobody was sad about it, also Tim died and Bob came back home from the cemetery crying



## Storyline
It’s Christmas Eve in London in 1843 and the old man Ebenezer Scrooge, a misley ﬁnancial expert, selﬁsh and grumpy, he rudely declines an invitation to dinner from his cheerful nephew Fred, his only living relative. 
Having reached the closing time of his studio, where he works together with his employee Bob Cratchit, who asks for the day off for Christmas but he refuses and also states that Christmas is a day like any other, after rudely chasing away two men who were asking him for a contribution for the needy, Scrooge goes alone to his abode. While he is eating, before going to bed, he is visited by the spirit of Jacob Marley, his old business partner, who died exactly seven years earlier on Christmas Eve. Marley's spirit appears to Scrooge surrounded by heavy chains from the apexes of which hang coffers: chains which, as Marley himself says, are the consequence of the greed and selﬁshness perpetrated while he was alive; he robbed widows and swindled the poor.
Scrooge, fearful, asks his old partner how he can avoid the same fate befalling him, so Marley indicates to Scrooge that there is still time to change his fate and, before taking his leave, announces to him that three spirits will come to visit him, that of Christmas past, Christmas present and Christmas future. The three ghosts, amid ﬂashbacks and frightening premonitions, will succeed in changing the old man's mean and selﬁsh nature, making him wake up on Christmas morning with the realization that the pursuit of money and attachment to material things alone are profoundly mistaken, and thus charity and brotherhood will ﬁnally make their way into the good heart of the old moneylender.
