
I'm absolutely thrilled and honored to announce that I've just received my author's copy of “The Revolution will not be litigated”.

A few years ago, my dear friend Katie Redford organized a groundbreaking meeting in Bellagio, where we delved into the powerful intersection of climate activism and the law. It was a transformative experience, and little did we know it would lead to something even more extraordinary.

Together with the brilliant Mark Gevisser, Katie has poured her heart and soul into editing a remarkable book that captures the essence of that gathering. Within the pages of the book, you'll find the stories of remarkable climate champions, such as Farhana Yamin and Ayisha Siddiqa, who have tirelessly fought for climate and social justice, as well as the inspiring journeys of activists like Ka Hsaw Wa and many others. Their voices deserve to be heard, celebrated, and amplified. 

And in the midst of all this, I'm humbled to have contributed to this invaluable collection. One of the highlights for me is a heartfelt conversation that took place in September 2019, involving myself, Eimar Sparks, Katie Redford, Farhana Yamin, and Mark Gevisser.

I want to express my deepest gratitude to Katie and Mark for their unwavering commitment, vision, and tireless efforts in bringing this book to life. Your dedication to amplifying the voices of climate activists is truly inspiring, and I feel honored to be a part of this monumental project.
